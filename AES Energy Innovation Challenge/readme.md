## Overview
This project was developed for the **AES Energy Innovation Challenge**, a global competition focused on sustainable energy solutions. Our team designed and analyzed an innovative system that improves the **thermal management of battery modules** and optimizes the **hydrogen fueling process** for next-generation clean energy applications.

## Objectives
- Design a **battery cooling system** with enhanced efficiency and safety.  
- Develop a **hydrogen fueling station concept** minimizing energy losses and emissions.  
- Integrate computational and experimental analysis to evaluate real-world performance.  
- Contribute to **energy transition goals** through practical and scalable engineering solutions.

## Methodology
1. **Thermal Simulation:** Conducted CFD simulations using *ANSYS Fluent* to analyze heat transfer and fluid flow behavior in battery modules.  
2. **Material & Fluid Selection:** Evaluated coolant types and materials for optimal thermal conductivity and chemical stability.  
3. **Hydrogen Fueling Design:** Modeled hydrogen compression, storage, and dispensing parameters for efficiency and safety.  
4. **Optimization:** Applied parametric studies to balance cost, temperature control, and reliability.  
5. **Validation:** Compared simulated results with literature and prototype testing data to ensure consistency.

## Results
- Achieved a **30% improvement in heat dissipation** compared to conventional air-cooling systems.  
- Demonstrated **significant reduction in charging time** and thermal runaway risk.  
- Proposed a **scalable hydrogen refueling concept** adaptable for future EV and industrial infrastructure.  
- Awarded **Third Place** among over 100 international teams for innovation and technical rigor.
